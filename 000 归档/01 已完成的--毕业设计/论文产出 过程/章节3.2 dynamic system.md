During two-point contact, dry sliding is observed during Z-axis motion. In the simulation, the maximum static friction coefficient is set as μ<sub>max</sub> = 0.45. Although this setting is not accurate, it is permitted since the friction coefficient in the virtual environment is only used to determine whether sliding occurs, rather than for detailed energy conservation analysis. The spring is uniformly distributed at three points on a circle with a diameter of 60 mm, and its stiffness is known to be _k_ = 16.3 N/mm. By adjusting the air pressure, the stiffness of the pneumatic-driven inductive micrometer is tuned to similar to that of the spring. Based on the pressure variation, the stiffness of inductive micrometer k_m can be estimated. The tilt stiffness of the springs κ0 is derived as κ<sub>0</sub> = (3/2)_kr_<sup>2</sup> = τ/θ. In the simulation, a coefficient of tilt stiffness _c_ = (3/2)_r_<sup>2</sup> is used, eliminating the need to explicitly define _k_ in the global settings. Under this configuration, it is necessary to assume that the spring length remains constant during peg-in-hole assembly. Due to the project limit of a maximum Z-axial force of 2 N, three springs with _k_ = 16.3 N/mm are employed, resulting in a maximum deformation of 41 μm. The Z-axis is advanced in 10 μm increments. If the Z-axial force variation reaches 2 N within four steps, it is concluded that jamming has occurred, and the torque variation should be obviously observed. The simulation is conducted to analyse ambiguous feedbacks. Therefore, the simulation does not require explicit modeling of spring compression.
For the quasi-static system, force equilibrium is maintained at each static moment. The Z-axial force _F<sub>z</sub>_, contact force _F<sub>t</sub>_, and static friction coefficient μ satisfy: μ_F<sub>t</sub>_ = _F<sub>z</sub>_. Furthermore, _F<sub>t</sub>_, insertion depth _h_, and torque τ are related by: _F<sub>t</sub>h_ = τ. Given the extremely small tilt angle θ, _F<sub>t</sub>_ is assumed to be purely radial. The tilt angle θ and torque τ follow: τ = θ_c_k. In the simulation system, _c_ (coefficient of tilt stiffness) is a predefined parameter. In each simulation, θ and _h_ are randomly generated. _F<sub>t</sub>_ is decomposed into _F<sub>x</sub>_ and _F<sub>y</sub>_ components. Based on these parameters, the theoretical data for six sensors are generated. In each simulation, six-step data are recorded and compiled into a table, as illustrated in Figure 3.2.
